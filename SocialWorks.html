Art is more than observation; it is a tool for change. This space is dedicated to initiatives that use the lens to support vulnerable communities and drive real impact.

The Reality: Child Poverty in Spain

Despite its status as a major European economy, Spain harbors a silent structural crisis. According to the latest AROPE (At Risk of Poverty or Social Exclusion) indicator, Spain consistently ranks among the countries with the highest child poverty rates in the European Union. Economic growth has unfortunately bypassed the most vulnerable families, leaving millions of minors exposed.

28.9%
Of children in Spain are at risk of poverty or social exclusion—over 2.1 million minors.
7%
Is the total poverty reduction achieved by Spain's social transfers, compared to the EU average of 15%.
1 in 3
Single-parent households (the vast majority led by women) face extreme financial vulnerability.

The Root of the Crisis

This crisis is fueled by a perfect storm of precarious employment, skyrocketing housing costs that consume over half of a vulnerable family's income, and an inadequate social protection system that fails to reach those who need it most. The result is an intergenerational cycle of inequality that compromises children's nutrition, mental health, and educational outcomes.

Sources: UNICEF Report on Child Poverty in Spain (2024); Save the Children AROPE Analysis.

The Project: Miradas de Calatayud

Since moving to Spain, the landscape and people of Calatayud have profoundly inspired my photography. "Miradas de Calatayud: Arte por la Infancia" is an upcoming charity exhibition designed to turn that artistic inspiration into tangible, structural help.

The concept is entirely transparent: I am donating my time, my portfolio, and the full rights to these images for this event. 100% of the net profits from the sale of the artwork will be donated directly to Save the Children Spain to fund their vital, on-the-ground programs combating child poverty and educational exclusion.

Save the Children has officially backed this initiative, and their logo will proudly be displayed as the sole beneficiary of the funds raised by the local community.
